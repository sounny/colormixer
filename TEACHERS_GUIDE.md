# Teacher's Guide: Color Mixer

Welcome to the **Color Mixer** educational tool! This app is designed to help students discover the fundamentals of color theory through hands-on experimentation. This guide provides lesson plans, discussion questions, and pedagogical strategies to maximize the learning impact in your classroom.

---

## üé® Learning Objectives

By using Color Mixer, students will be able to:
1.  **Differentiate** between **Subtractive (Paint/RYB)** and **Additive (Light/RGB)** color models.
2.  **Identify** Primary colors for both models.
3.  **Predict** the resulting Secondary colors when mixing two Primaries.
4.  **Understand** why mixing all paint colors results in dark colors (absorption) while mixing all light colors results in white (emission).

## üè´ Lesson Plans

### 1. Hands-On Discovery (Grades K-2)
**Goal**: Identify primary and secondary paint colors.
*   **Activity**: Open the app in **Paint (RYB)** mode. Ask students to click each color one by one. Then, ask them to find three different ways to make a "new" color using only two clicks.
*   **Challenge**: Use the **Challenge Mode** (Game icon) and see who can reach "Level 2" first!
*   **Discussion**: "Which mix was your favorite? Does mixing more colors make the result lighter or darker?"

### 2. The Science of Light (Grades 3-5)
**Goal**: Explore the RGB model and compare it to paint.
*   **Activity**: Switch to **Light (RGB)** mode. Ask students to mix Red and Green. Many will expect Brown or Orange (like paint), but they will discover **Yellow**.
*   **Comparison**: Have students make "Yellow" in both Paint and Light modes. Discuss the difference in the "ingredients" needed.
*   **Deep Dive**: Use the **"Learn Why"** tooltips to read the scientific explanations for each mix.

### 3. Color Expert Challenge (Grades 6-8)
**Goal**: Master the relationship between all color combinations.
*   **Activity**: Start the **Learning Path** (Book icon). Have students complete the entire 9-step guided sequence.
*   **Project**: Ask students to find a real-world object (e.g., a blade of grass, a sunset) and recreate that color in the app using both modes if possible.
*   **Outcome**: Students should be able to explain why a computer screen (RGB) uses different primaries than a desktop printer (CMYK/RYB concepts).

*   **Scaffolding**: Encourage younger students to start with the **Learning Path**. It provides step-by-step instructions that "fade" as they gain confidence.
*   **Gamification**: Use the **Achievement System** to motivate students. Ask them to "Unlock the Color Expert badge" as a homework or in-class goal.
